Mohsin Naqvi: Who Is He?

Naqvi was born on October 28, 1978, in Lahore, Punjab. He received his primary education from Crescent Model School, Lahore.1

Later, he went on to attend Government College University (GCU) in Lahore, where he completed his graduation in media sciences.

After completing his studies, Naqvi moved to the United States to further explore the field of media.

He stayed in Miami, a coastal metropolis, and joined the multinational cable news giant, CNN, as a correspondent. This experience helped him to gain a broader perspective on the workings of the media industry and to expand his skillset.

Returning to Pakistan, Naqvi made history by becoming the youngest Regional Head of South Asia at CNN. He continued to work there for some time before deciding to start his own media venture.

Naqvi continues to inspire and motivate young media professionals to follow their dreams and to work towards achieving their goals. His contribution to the media industry in Pakistan has been significant, and his legacy will undoubtedly continue to inspire generations to come.

Mohsin Naqvi Founded City Media Group

In 2009, Naqvi founded City Media Group, which has since grown to become one of the largest media groups in Pakistan.

The first channel launched by the group was C42, which was later rebranded as City 42. The channel became very popular, and it wasn’t long before Naqvi expanded his business, launching several other channels.

Today, Naqvi runs a total of six TV channels and one newspaper.

The channels include 24 News Channel, Rohi Channel for Sairiki Belt, popular channel of Faisalabad city C41, and Channel 21 from Karachi. His Urdu News Channel, C44, is also working in the UK.

Naqvi’s success story is a testament to his passion for media and his commitment to excellence. He has shown that with hard work, dedication, and a clear vision, it is possible to achieve great things in the media industry.

Mohsin Naqvi: Appointment As Caretaker CM Of Punjab

A petition has been filed in Pakistan’s Supreme Court challenging the appointment of senior journalist Mohsin Raza Naqvi as the caretaker chief minister of Punjab by the Election Commission of Pakistan (ECP).3

This move has come in response to Naqvi’s name being proposed by the Opposition Leader in the Punjab Assembly, Hamza Shehbaz.

In addition, former Prime Minister and Chairman of the Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI), as well as his key ally, ex-Chief Minister of Punjab Chaudhry Pervaiz Elahi, have announced their intention to challenge the appointment in the apex court.

PTI Secretary General Asad Umar has filed the petition calling upon the top court to stop Naqvi from working as the caretaker chief minister.

The petitioner has also challenged the appointment of the members of the ECP as well as the Opposition Leader in the National Assembly, Raja Riaz.

The federal government, the National Assembly Speaker Raja Pervez Ashraf, Leader of the Opposition Raja Riaz, the governments of Punjab and Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KP), the Election Commission of Pakistan, and Punjab caretaker CM Mohsin Naqvi have all been made parties in the petition.

The petition argues that Mohsin Naqvi lacks the necessary “intellectual and relative experience as well as the characteristics” needed to be appointed at the top slot in the province.

Furthermore, the petitioner claims that some equal or better-suited candidates were available but were not considered consciously.

The petition also states that Naqvi has no political, administrative, or legal experience in ensuring the rule of law, the working of governments, bureaucracy, the requirements of law, and/or the Constitution.

Challenges to Naqvi’s appointment are being brought to light as concerns about the impartiality of caretaker setups in Pakistan continue to grow.

These setups are appointed to oversee the smooth transition of power from one government to another in the lead-up to general elections. Critics argue that the process is open to abuse and that the caretaker government often fails to conduct free and fair elections.
