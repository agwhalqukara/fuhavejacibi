Early Life

This legendary musician was born in Los Angeles, California. His father was Floyd Crosby, who won the academy award for cinematography. His mother belonged to the famous Van Cortlandt family. He changed many schools. He went to different schools like University Elementary School, Laguna Blanca School, and Crane Country Day School.

At Crane Country Day School, he performed in many musicals. In 1960 his parents got divorced, and his father married again. At Santa Barbara City College, Crosby studied drama. Later he joined the troop of a black singer named Terry Callier in Chicago. Producer Jim Dickson helped him to record his first solo.
